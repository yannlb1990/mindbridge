# Clinical Terminology Guide

## Overview
This document defines the psychological and clinical terminology used throughout the MindBridge platform. The clinician interface uses professional terminology, while the client app uses accessible, non-clinical language.

## Assessment Instruments

### Depression & Anxiety

#### PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)
- **Purpose**: Screen and measure depression severity
- **Scoring**: 0-27 (0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-19 moderately severe, 20-27 severe)
- **Frequency**: Typically at intake, then every 2-4 weeks
- **Client language**: "Mood check-in" or "How you've been feeling"

#### GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7)
- **Purpose**: Screen and measure anxiety severity
- **Scoring**: 0-21 (0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-21 severe)
- **Frequency**: At intake, then every 2-4 weeks
- **Client language**: "Worry and anxiety check-in"

#### K10 (Kessler Psychological Distress Scale)
- **Purpose**: Measure psychological distress
- **Scoring**: 10-50 (10-15 low, 16-21 moderate, 22-29 high, 30-50 very high)
- **Common in**: Australian Medicare assessments
- **Client language**: "Wellbeing check"

#### DASS-21 (Depression Anxiety Stress Scales)
- **Purpose**: Measures depression, anxiety, and stress
- **Scoring**: Three subscales, each 0-42
- **Client language**: "Mood and stress check"

### Eating Disorders

#### EDE-Q (Eating Disorder Examination Questionnaire)
- **Purpose**: Assess eating disorder psychopathology
- **Subscales**: Restraint, Eating Concern, Shape Concern, Weight Concern
- **Scoring**: Global score 0-6
- **Frequency**: Monthly or at key treatment points
- **Client language**: "Eating and body image check-in"

#### CIA (Clinical Impairment Assessment)
- **Purpose**: Measure functional impairment from ED
- **Client language**: "How eating affects your life"

### Child & Adolescent

#### RCADS (Revised Child Anxiety and Depression Scale)
- **Purpose**: Anxiety and depression in children/adolescents
- **Versions**: Child (age 8-11), Youth (age 11-18), Parent
- **Client language**: "How you've been feeling lately"

#### SDQ (Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire)
- **Purpose**: Behavioural screening in children/adolescents
- **Versions**: Self, Parent, Teacher
- **Client language**: "About me" questionnaire

#### SCAS (Spence Children's Anxiety Scale)
- **Purpose**: Anxiety symptoms in children
- **Client language**: "Worries and fears check-in"

### Risk Assessment

#### Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)
- **Purpose**: Suicide risk assessment
- **Use**: Structured risk evaluation
- **Never client-facing**: Clinician tool only

#### NSSI Assessment (Non-Suicidal Self-Injury)
- **Purpose**: Assess self-harm behaviours
- **Use**: Clinical assessment
- **Sensitive handling**: Training required

## Mental Status Examination (MSE) Terms

### Appearance
- Grooming: Well-groomed, disheveled, unkempt
- Attire: Appropriate, inappropriate, bizarre
- Physical: Thin, emaciated, underweight, normal weight

### Behaviour
- Psychomotor: Agitation, retardation, normal
- Eye contact: Good, poor, avoidant, intense
- Rapport: Good, guarded, hostile, cooperative

### Speech
- Rate: Normal, pressured, slow
- Volume: Normal, loud, soft
- Quality: Fluent, dysarthric, monotone

### Mood (Client's Report)
- Subjective state: "Anxious", "sad", "okay", "fine"

### Affect (Clinician Observation)
- Quality: Euthymic, depressed, anxious, irritable, labile
- Range: Full, restricted, blunted, flat
- Congruence: Mood-congruent, mood-incongruent
- Reactivity: Reactive, non-reactive

### Thought Content
- Suicidal ideation: Present/absent, passive/active, plan, intent
- Homicidal ideation: Present/absent
- Delusions: Persecutory, grandiose, etc. (rare in this population)
- Obsessions: Intrusive, ego-dystonic thoughts

### Thought Process
- Flow: Logical, circumstantial, tangential, loose
- Speed: Normal, racing, slowed

### Perception
- Hallucinations: Auditory, visual (rare)
- Dissociative experiences

### Cognition
- Orientation: Person, place, time
- Concentration: Intact, impaired
- Memory: Intact, impaired

### Insight
- Level: Good, fair, poor, absent

### Judgment
- Quality: Good, fair, poor, impaired

## Risk Assessment Terminology

### Suicidal Ideation
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| None | No thoughts of suicide |
| Passive | Wishes to be dead, no intent |
| Active, no plan | Thoughts of killing self, no specific plan |
| Active with plan | Has identified method/means |
| Imminent | Immediate intent, requires intervention |

### Self-Harm
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| NSSI | Non-Suicidal Self-Injury (e.g., cutting without suicidal intent) |
| DSH | Deliberate Self-Harm (broader term) |
| Frequency | Isolated, episodic, chronic |
| Recency | Current, recent (<1 month), historical |

### Eating Disorder Medical Risk
| Indicator | Concern Level |
|-----------|--------------|
| BMI <15 | Severe (medical monitoring required) |
| BMI 15-17.5 | Moderate |
| Heart rate <50 | Medical risk |
| Orthostatic changes | Medical risk |
| Electrolyte imbalance | Medical emergency |
| Rapid weight loss | Monitoring required |

## Progress Note Terminology

### Session Structure
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Presenting concerns | What the client brought to session |
| Session content | What was discussed/worked on |
| Interventions | Techniques/strategies used |
| Client response | How client engaged with interventions |
| Risk assessment | Current risk status |
| Plan | Next steps and goals |

### Common Phrases
| Clinical Phrase | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| "Client presented as..." | How client appeared |
| "Reports subjective mood as..." | Client's self-reported mood |
| "Demonstrated insight into..." | Showed understanding of... |
| "Psychoeducation provided regarding..." | Education given about... |
| "Cognitive restructuring techniques utilised..." | Challenged unhelpful thoughts |
| "Homework assigned..." | Between-session tasks given |
| "Treatment goals reviewed..." | Progress towards goals discussed |
| "Risk assessed as..." | Current risk level determined |

## Client Communication Guidelines

### Words to Avoid
| Clinical Term | Use Instead |
|--------------|-------------|
| "Patient" | "Client" or name |
| "Non-compliant" | "Not yet ready" or "finding it difficult" |
| "Failed" | "Didn't work this time" |
| "Anorexic/bulimic" (as identity) | "Experiencing anorexia/bulimia" |
| "Commit suicide" | "Die by suicide" |
| "Symptoms" (in client app) | "Experiences" or "challenges" |
| "Disorder" (in client app) | "Difficulty" or "challenge" |

### Encouraging Language
- "You're making progress"
- "Recovery is possible"
- "Every step counts"
- "It's okay to struggle sometimes"
- "You're not alone in this"
