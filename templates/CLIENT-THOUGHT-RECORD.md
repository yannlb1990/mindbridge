### What is this exercise?

Thought records help you notice and challenge unhelpful thoughts. When we're struggling, our minds can play tricks on us - making things seem worse than they are. This exercise helps you look at your thoughts from a different angle.

## Tips for Using Thought Records

1. **Do it in the moment** (or as soon as you can after)
2. **Be specific** - general thoughts are harder to challenge
3. **Look for patterns** - do certain thoughts come up often?
4. **Practice makes progress** - this gets easier with time
5. **It's okay if you still feel upset** - the goal isn't to feel perfect

---

## Common Thinking Traps to Watch For

| Trap | What it looks like |
|------|-------------------|
| **All-or-nothing** | "I ate one biscuit, so I've ruined everything" |
| **Mind reading** | "Everyone thinks I'm fat" |
| **Fortune telling** | "I'll definitely lose control if I eat that" |
| **Catastrophising** | "If I eat this, something terrible will happen" |
| **Should statements** | "I should be able to handle this by now" |
| **Emotional reasoning** | "I feel fat, so I must be fat" |
| **Filtering** | Only noticing the negative, ignoring positives |
